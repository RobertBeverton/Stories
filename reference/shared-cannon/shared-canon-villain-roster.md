# Villain Roster & Casting Notes
## Felix & Alex Story Universe

> Companion to *felix_alex_story_generation_guide.md*. Every villain here follows the anatomy rule from the guide: **Visual → Scheme → Reason → Moment → Resolution → Afterlife.** Use this doc to keep returning villains consistent and to avoid accidentally repeating a gimmick.

---

## The Core Rule

**Villains are sad before they are bad. Always. No exceptions.**

Every memorable villain in this universe loses happiness, feels underestimated, or gets robbed of something — and acts out because of it. Five-year-olds understand this instinctively. Don't skip the sadness to get to the scheme.

## The Thursday Support Group Roster (current, as of Case File #29+)

*Victor Spung · Victoria Vance · Voldemort · Arthur (Mondays) · Caspian Webb · The Halloween Troll · Nell (Null) · Gerald Pritt · Claudia Nix · The Amplifier*

The group is real and warm — **not a joke.** It meets Thursdays, Janet does the shortbread, and it's genuinely where people go to get better. New villains being sent there by Felix ("There's a support group") is a running gag *and* carries real warmth — keep both halves.

Once someone joins the group, **they don't go fully bad again.** They can slip. They come back.

## Villain Profiles

### Victor Spung ("Victoria Sponge")
- **Appearances:** Case File #17 (intro), #19 (Vance alliance)
- **Visual:** Large, greenish, slightly scaly, an enormous billowing cloak he genuinely loves
- **Scheme:** Originally just wanted to win the village fête cake competition but wasn't allowed to enter — looked too intimidating
- **Reason:** Lonely; wanted people to not be frightened of him
- **Moment:** Turning up to a villain alliance still wearing his first-prize ribbon and not seeing why that's strange
- **Resolution/Afterlife:** Regular at Thursday group. Baking has improved enormously. Wears the ribbon on his cloak.
- **Casting note style (for future voice profiles):** Large, warm-hearted physical presence; earnest rather than menacing once you look past the cloak.

### Victoria Vance
- **Appearances:** Case File #19 (Spung alliance)
- **Scheme:** Formed an alliance with Victor Spung after meeting at the Thursday-adjacent general support group
- **Reason:** Both had grudges and ambitions and felt underestimated
- **Moment:** Cried "in a good way" when Victor brought a Victoria sponge to their third session together
- **Afterlife:** Ongoing — the alliance itself became comic rather than threatening

### Voldemort
- **Recurring across multiple case files**
- **Scheme escalation:** 3 inches → full-size → giant, across separate returns — each escalated stakes without contradicting the last
- **Growth signs:** Attends the Thursday group *early* now, not as a last resort. Sends postscript "cards" (e.g., "adequate work," "adequate shortbread") that read as dry affection rather than menace.
- **Rule demonstrated:** Returning villains should escalate, but always show small signs of growth alongside the escalation.

### The Halloween Troll
- **Scheme:** Stole Halloween sweets / caused a happiness-draining problem
- **Reason:** Had never laughed — not regressed, but robbed of the chance
- **Resolution:** Keeps one toffee
- **Note:** Model example of "villain has a specific sadness that maps to something a 5-year-old understands" — loneliness, embarrassment, grief made concrete

### Arthur (Mondays)
- **Gimmick:** Associated with the "17 Mondays" story object — a jar containing grey-blue, Monday-coloured time
- **Story seed still open:** What actually happens inside the jar has never been explored (see Story Seeds in the generation guide)

### Gerald Pritt (the miniaturiser)
- **Flagged in the guide as the weakest villain** — competent and efficient but emotionally thin
- **Fix used:** A single humanising line ("he's been in a tiny office for fifteen years") lifted him considerably
- **Lesson:** Even a thin villain needs at minimum one sentence of interior life

### Claudia Nix
- Thursday group member; details not yet expanded in the material reviewed — open for development.

### The Amplifier
- Thursday group member; mechanism/backstory not yet detailed — open for development.

### The Grey King (Sweet Land)
- **World:** Sweet Land (see Story Worlds doc)
- **Scheme:** Could not stand sweetness of any kind — not just sweets, but laughter, kindness, sunshine. Took everything sweet from Sweet Land, turning it grey.
- **Visual:** Over hundreds of years turned completely grey — grey skin, grey clothes, grey eyes
- **Reason:** Implied loss of his own capacity for sweetness/joy over a very long time
- **Moment/Resolution:** Transformed through Felix offering his hand — one of the strongest emotional beats in the whole collection
- **Do not reuse this precise mechanism** (offered hand undoing total transformation) without deliberately building toward something as earned — it's a high-water mark, not a template to repeat casually

### The Architect (Case File #30 — ongoing, meta-villain)
- **Not a standard villain — treat separately and carefully**
- Has been supplying magical items to troubled people for 31 years, purely to watch what they do
- Was completely unaware of Trio Force; is now aware, and *curious* rather than hostile
- Not redeemable in the usual sense — he never hurt anyone directly, he gave people power and watched. The moral question is genuinely complex and shouldn't be resolved simply.
- **Handling rules:**
  - Do not resolve too quickly — this is series-finale energy, to be earned slowly
  - Each Architect-planted item on the Evidence Shelf should eventually be revealed retroactively ("oh, THAT came from him")
  - When they finally meet properly, **Felix should say the thing that matters** — that's his role in this universe
  - Latest status (Case File #31): the Architect is watching Theo's story specifically and privately noted "unexpected" — the most interesting thing to him in 31 years

## Casting Note Format (for future villains)

When adding a new villain, write two separate blocks:

**Character description** — personality, visual, scheme, reason, moment, resolution, afterlife (per the anatomy table above).

**Voice casting note** (separate paragraph, for read-aloud/TTS use), covering:
- Pitch (deep baritone / mid-range / high and reedy)
- Speech rate (slow and deliberate / normal / fast and erratic)
- Emotional register (cold and flat / theatrical / quietly threatening / excitable and unhinged)
- Distinctive vocal quirks (whispers, trails off, sudden laughs, over-enunciates)
- Age and wear (young / middle-aged / elderly; smooth, gravelly, strained)
- Accent or origin (optional)

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid (from what didn't work)

- **No emotional dimension** — a villain who's only "competent and efficient" reads flat (Gerald Pritt's original write-up)
- **Too many villains in one story** — keep to one clear mechanism per story; even alliances (Vance + Spung) should still feel like one combined scheme, not two
- **Undoing a redemption** — nobody who's joined the Thursday group should go fully bad again
