## The Twins

> **UPDATED — the equal braid (supersedes the older "Felix does / Alex notices").** Both twins are creative and neither is a function of the other. Alex is NOT merely a sensor. This is the current model across all series; the grounded Bramble Wall series uses it strictly, and it should be the default everywhere.

### Felix — age 5
- **Role:** Twin 1, co-lead
- **Traits:** Loud, brave, impulsive, enormous heart, acts first, right more than he should be
- **Story engine — IMAGINATION:** Felix invents the world and the mission ("this is the base / we're iron-hunters / there's treasure"). He drives the story's *want*. He does and jumps and says the brave thing.
- **Signature line quality:** Says the important thing simply. Cuts straight to it.
- **Real-world note:** cannot ride a bike and isn't interested — don't "fix" this.
- **Powers skew (magic universe):** Active, physical, loud — flying, magnetism, time-stop, invisibility, jumping, gravity control, warmth

### Alex — age 5
- **Role:** Twin 2, co-lead
- **Traits:** Quiet, observant, patient, calm in a crisis, says less and means more; excellent fine/gross motor skill
- **Story engine — EXECUTION:** Alex makes it real. He finishes what Felix starts and makes things actually work and hold; he stays with a fiddly job long after others quit. He notices too — but noticing is texture, not his whole role. He has his OWN wants.
- **Signature line quality:** Few words, real weight.
- **Real-world note:** has an electric trials bike (OSET 16R) — his solo domain; may rarely *choose* to share it, and it means something when he does.
- **Powers skew (magic universe):** Perceptive, quiet, precise — stillness, size-changing, truth-mirror, touch-history, calm-field

**The braid:** Felix imagines it; Alex makes it real. Neither works without the other. Both are creative acts.
**The golden rule:** Felix and Alex are never interchangeable. If Alex disappears for a page, something's gone wrong with the story.

**Note on cousins' role:** Per the generation guide, Elfine and Marion are listed as extended cast who **"never appeared in a story yet"** as of the guide's writing — but the conversation history shows they've actually starred in a number of earlier, looser stories (treasure hunts, the "7 Club," Super Kid Squad). Those earlier stories sit in a slightly different, more freeform continuity than the polished Trio Force universe. Worth a decision: fold the cousins properly into Trio Force canon as a real story arc, or keep them as a separate, simpler "cousin visits" strand. See *Open Questions* at the end of this doc.
