# Story Worlds & Locations Atlas
## Felix & Alex Story Universe

> Companion to *felix_alex_story_generation_guide.md*. A map of every place the stories have used — the home base, the countryside, the town, and the special "other worlds" reached through a portal or invitation. Use this to decide whether a new story stays grounded (Character Adventure format) or opens a doorway (Standalone Fantasy Adventure format).

## Other Worlds (Reached via Portal / Invitation)

These are one-off or recurring "somewhere else" settings used for the bigger Standalone Fantasy Adventures. Treat each as a self-contained set-piece rather than a place the twins can casually pop back to (unless a story deliberately reopens the door).

### Sweet Land
- **How they got there:** Ginny Gingerbread, a messenger, arrived and led them through via a glowing golden sweet, holding hands
- **Landscape:** Chocolate river, lollipop forest, fizzy sea, biscuit-crumb ground, candy-cane trees — all turning grey under the Grey King's influence
- **Journey structure used:** Fizzy Sea (wafer boat) → Toffee Cliffs (sticky, slow climb) → Grey King's mountain
- **Known issue (per the guide):** the middle section had three near-identical obstacle stops — the rule going forward is **maximum two obstacle stops** on any journey
- **Status:** Resolved (Grey King transformed) — a strong candidate for a **sequel visit**, now that Sweet Land is restored, rather than a repeat rescue

### Blackwood Forest (Pirates of Blackwood Forest)
- **How it started:** Daddy left a rolled-up treasure map, tied with string, propped against the fruit bowl — claimed to know nothing about it
- **Structure:** Clue-by-clue treasure hunt through the big forest, ending at a real chest
- **Chest contents:** Chocolate coins, real compasses, bandanas, a pocket telescope, and a letter from Daddy
- **Payoff location:** The oak tree clearing
- **Status:** The gold-standard example of the Standalone Fantasy Adventure format — the benchmark to measure new full adventures against

### Turtle Island
- A small pond-island reached by rowing boat, containing a buried tin box of family-history treasure (coins from a great-great-grandfather) rather than magic — a good model for a **non-magical, family-history-flavoured** treasure hunt

### The Halloween World / Troll's Domain
- Where the Halloween Troll operates; overlaps with the town during Halloween-specific stories
- Central object: a happiness-draining machine

## Format-to-World Matching Guide

| If the story is... | Use... |
|---|---|
| A quick bedtime case file | Stay in HQ / town / forest — no portal needed |
| A character adventure (smaller cast) | The field, the bridge, the forest, Broken Cove — grounded in their real countryside |
| A full special-occasion adventure | A portal world (Sweet Land, Blackwood Forest) or a big real-world set piece (Turtle Island) |
| A three-part epic | Reserve for well-established worlds/villains only (e.g. the Architect arc) — not a first-time location |
