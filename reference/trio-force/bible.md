# Trio Force — Series Bible (canonical)

*The single source of truth for the Trio Force universe, RESTARTED. Merges the fixed frame, survival principles, and the open slate. Supersedes the old, uncurated back-catalogue's numbering and Amelia's role — see PART H for the patch/retirement notes.*

*Companion (wider universe): `reference/shared-cannon/shared-canon-character-directory.md`, `shared-canon-generation-guide.md`, `shared-canon-magic-and-powers-system.md`, `shared-canon-villain-roster.md`, `felix-alex-story-archive.md`. This series IS the magic/powers universe — the counterweight to the grounded, no-magic Bramble Wall series.*

---

## PART A — WHAT THIS SERIES IS

**Felix and Alex are Trio Force — two five-year-olds with a garden shed HQ, gold question-mark boxes that hand out powers matched to who they are, and a rotating cast of villains who turn out to be sad rather than evil.**

The engine is *a case arrives → powers arrive → the boys investigate and fix it themselves → the villain joins the Thursday group.* Unlike Bramble Wall (one place, one real forest, grounded), Trio Force is **episodic and open-ended** — cases can go anywhere (the field, the town, the forest, occasionally somewhere stranger) and the "world" grows through recurring institutions (HQ, the Thursday group, the evidence shelf) rather than through one physical location.

It is **not a finite set** — new case files are written as wanted, the way the original ~40-story back-catalogue was. What's new this time is a **clean numbering restart and a disciplined bible**, so the series doesn't re-accumulate the same drift (three different "Case File #33"s, an inconsistent Amelia, a stale non-negotiable) that the old catalogue had.

## PART B — SURVIVAL PRINCIPLES

*What keeps this series robust against its actual, demonstrated failure modes — drawn directly from auditing the ~40-story back-catalogue.*

1. **One canonical number line, assigned only at LOCK, never at draft.** The old catalogue's numbering collided because multiple drafts from the same or different chat sessions all claimed adjacent numbers informally. Going forward: a case file is only assigned its number in `ledgers.md` once it is locked. Never write "Case File #12" into a draft before that draft is actually locked.
2. **Amelia is a resource, not a cast member.** The old catalogue put a 13-year-old's literary sensibility (a notebook, "writes things true," underlining) into a story for 5-year-olds, and it read older than the audience every time it leaned on her. **Fix, locked with the user:** Amelia now appears only as an occasional off-screen check — the boys radio or run next door to ask her to *confirm* something they already suspect (what a sound was, whether she saw someone, what a word means) — never as a co-investigator on scene, never carrying a notebook on-page. See PART C §2.
3. **Every case stands FULLY alone; interlocking elements REWARD, never gate.** The evidence shelf, the Thursday group, a returning villain's growth — these reward a listener who's heard others, but nobody should need to have heard a previous case to follow this one. Re-ground shared elements in one light line, never assume prior cases were heard.
4. **Establish LAZILY.** Case File #1 (this one) establishes only what it needs: the boys, HQ, the box mechanic, ONE villain, the Thursday group as an idea. It does NOT need to explain the evidence shelf's full history, or seed the Architect-style meta-arc, or introduce Amelia's full backstory. Later cases earn their own additions.
5. **Villains are sad before they are bad, always, no exceptions** — the single most consistent and most successful element of the old catalogue (confirmed across every story audited). Keep it as the load-bearing emotional engine.
6. **Powers have a stated limit at the moment they're granted.** Also the most consistent, most successful mechanical rule in the old catalogue. Keep it exactly as-is.
7. **The parents are true-but-background, per `craft-standing-rules.md` — NOT the old "Robert makes tea, always" sitcom version.** The old generation guide's Non-Negotiable #10 is retired (see PART H). Use the same Dad/Magda established in Bramble Wall's canon patch — they're the same real parents in both series, just background here too.

## PART C — THE FIXED FRAME (constant across every case file)

### 1. The two leads — the braid (per `craft-standing-rules.md`)
- **Felix (5)** — imagination/the want engine. Loud, brave, impulsive, acts first, says the important thing simply. His powers skew active/physical/loud.
- **Alex (5)** — the doing/holding engine, not a mere "noticer." Quiet, precise, calm in a crisis, few words, real weight. His powers skew perceptive/precise/quiet.
- **The braid:** Felix imagines the plan; Alex is the one who actually makes the fix work. Neither solves it alone. Each has his OWN want in every case, not just a shared one.
- **Golden rule:** if Alex disappears for a page, something's wrong.

### 2. Amelia — the verifier (RESTORED ROLE, not a cast member)
- 13, next door, sensible, wry. **Does NOT appear on-scene at HQ or in the field.** She is a **phone-a-friend**: the boys radio her, or one of them nips next door for thirty seconds, to check ONE concrete fact they already have a hunch about ("Did you hear anything by the bridge last night?" / "What's a word that means someone's showing off but doesn't mean it nastily?"). She gives a short, real answer and that's it — she never joins the investigation, never carries a notebook on-page, never explains the plan back to them.
- **Why this fix, and how to apply it:** the old version had her present throughout, planning and writing in a notebook — a 13-year-old's register bleeding into a story for two 5-year-olds. Demoting her to an occasional, useful, off-screen voice keeps the warmth of "we know someone older who helps" without importing her voice into the boys' scenes. **Use her AT MOST once per case file, briefly.** Many case files can skip her entirely — she is not required per case.

### 3. HQ — the garden shed that isn't a shed
- Across the field from the house — reachable on foot in a couple of minutes.
- Has **two desks** (Felix's and Alex's — not three; Amelia doesn't have one, per §2), a **snack drawer** ("not for visitors," per the sign), and an **evidence shelf** that grows, case by case, with one object per resolved case.
- Boxes arrive **on the desks or the doorstep**, gold, with a shimmering question mark, on mornings when something is about to matter.

### 4. Powers — the gold-box mechanic
- **Delivery:** Mario-style gold question-mark boxes. One per person, always, at the very START of the case, before the investigation — so the power gets tested, misused a bit for comedy, and then actually used to solve the case.
- **Match personality:** Felix → active/physical/loud. Alex → perceptive/quiet/precise. When a power deliberately breaks this pattern, the story should treat it as a noticed surprise, not a silent inconsistency.
- **Always a stated limit,** given at the moment the power is granted (a count, a duration, a condition). The limit is where the tension and the joke both live.
- **Powers can persist across cases** as a *reward* for continuity listeners, but no case may assume a prior power is remembered — re-state briefly if reused.

### 5. The villain — sad before bad
- **Anatomy (per `shared-canon-villain-roster.md`, kept as-is — it's the strongest element of the old catalogue):** Visual (one concrete, slightly silly detail) → Scheme (one clear mechanism a 5-year-old can explain back) → Reason (a real sadness or frustration underneath) → Moment (one scene where they're just a person) → Resolution (they keep one small thing) → Afterlife (Thursday group, or at least a postscript).
- **Resolution is always through understanding, not defeat.** The boys' powers help them get close enough or reveal the truth — they don't "beat" the villain in a fight.
- **Nobody who joins the Thursday group goes fully bad again.** They can slip; they come back.

### 6. The Thursday group
- A real, warm, recurring institution — reformed and reforming villains meet Thursdays. **Janet makes shortbread** (running gag: her backhanded-compliment reviews of new attendees' baking — "professionally threatening," etc). Referenced lightly in most cases; never mocked as a joke-only device.
- New villains being sent there ("There's a support group") is a running line, said by whichever character earns it that case (usually Felix).

### 7. The evidence shelf
- One item added per resolved case, kept in the shed. A physical, growing memory of the series — mirrors Bramble Wall's windowsill shelf in function, but belongs to the case files rather than one family home.
- Each item should be nameable in one phrase (a stolen-colour jar, a compass that points to feelings, a snow-globe house) — see `ledgers.md` for the running inventory.

### 8. The physical object at the centre
- Every case, per `craft-standing-rules.md`, needs one physical object a child can picture and want to hold. Often this doubles as the evidence-shelf addition.

### 9. Tone, craft & values (constant)
- Concrete sensory detail, sound-effect words, physical comedy, repetition-with-variation, fairness/kindness as felt values.
- **Don't let the narrator say the quiet part out loud** — the universe's characteristic failure mode; watch every pass.
- Affirmations woven, never bolted on (~2–3 seams per case).
- One laugh and one feeling per ending, in that order where possible.
- Cast ceiling: **6 speaking characters max**, 4 active is more comfortable. With Amelia now off-screen-by-default, most cases will run Felix + Alex + 1 villain (+ maybe one guest) — comfortably under the ceiling.

### 10. Length / format
- **Trio Force Case File format** (per `shared-canon-generation-guide.md`'s table, still valid): **900–1,500 words / 7–10 min narrated** is the default. A "quick case file" (600–800w) is fine for a lighter night. Longer "full adventure" format (1,500–2,000w) is a different, occasional format — not the Case File default.

## PART D — THE LIVE DIALS (what each case file may vary)

| Dial | Range |
|---|---|
| Where the case happens | the field, the town, the forest edge, occasionally somewhere stranger reached through the case itself |
| The villain and their scheme | anything with ONE clear, concrete mechanism |
| Powers granted | new each case, matched to personality, always with a limit |
| Guest cast | none / one school friend or cousin / Amelia (off-screen only, ≤1 use) |
| Whether the Thursday group is referenced | lightly, most cases; can be skipped |
| Evidence-shelf addition | whatever physical object anchors this case |
| Tone | comedy-adventure ↔ gentle-sad-villain warmth; not every case is deep |

## PART E — THE SLATE (open-ended; Case File #1 locks the pattern)

Unlike Bramble Wall's closed 5-book arc, Trio Force is an **open slate** — case files are added as written. This section tracks only what's committed so far.

### CASE FILE #1 — *The Grumble Who Stole the Colours* *(origin — locks the pattern)*
- **Felix's want:** something in the field has gone visibly wrong (colour draining from it) and he wants to go and sort it out *now* — today, before more of it disappears.
- **Alex's want:** to understand *why* it's happening before rushing in — he's the one who notices the pattern in how the grey is spreading.
- **Villain:** a Grumble living under the stone bridge at the bottom of the field — sad, not evil; eats colour because it can't hold onto anything and keeps trying, keeps failing, keeps needing more.
- **Powers:** two gold boxes, one each, at HQ. Felix's is active/physical; Alex's is perceptive/precise. Each with a stated limit from the moment it's granted.
- **HQ established:** the shed, two desks, the snack drawer, the very first evidence-shelf item.
- **Amelia:** does NOT appear on-scene. If used at all, one brief off-screen radio check, no notebook, no writing-things-true power.
- **Villain afterlife:** stays in its home (under the bridge); one small sign it's welcome to visit the Thursday group later, without over-promising a sequel.
